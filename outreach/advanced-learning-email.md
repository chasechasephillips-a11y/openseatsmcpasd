Hi all —

Quick note. Most of you know I've been working with [parent group / name] on
the advanced-learning identification piece. This email is about a parallel
effort that I think a lot of you will recognize as the same underlying problem:
**MCPASD's governance hasn't kept up with what families actually need.**

The petition I've been circulating proposes a small structural change to the
school board: open 4 of the 9 seats to any qualified district resident, keep 5
anchored to Areas. Same board size. Voters still pick every seat. The only
thing that changes is *who's allowed to be on the ballot.*

Why it matters for our group specifically: when every seat is locked to a
geographic Area, advocacy on a district-wide issue — like identification
criteria, like equity of access to advanced programming — has to thread itself
through whichever board member happens to live near the problem. A more open
ballot means a deeper bench of people who can run on the issues we actually
care about, from anywhere in the district.

The site has the full case: https://openseatsmcpasd.org

## A few things people get hung up on (so you can pre-empt)

- **"Is this partisan?"** No. No PAC, no party, parents pay for printing.
- **"Will my Area lose its voice?"** No. Every Area keeps a permanent anchor
  seat. Today Area IV has 4 seats, others have 1 — this makes it equal.
- **"Will any current member lose their seat?"** No. Conversion happens only
  at natural re-election (2027, 2028, 2030).
- **"Is it legal?"** Yes — Wis. Stat. § 120.02(2)(a) explicitly authorizes
  this. Counsel-reviewed.

If anyone hits you with a question you don't want to wing, just point them at
the FAQ — it covers everything: https://openseatsmcpasd.org/toolkit/faq.html

## Notes on using this draft

- **Personalize the opener** — drop in the actual parent-group name and any
  specific person/initiative reference so it reads like a peer email, not a
  blast.
- **Update the tally before sending** — pull the live count from the
  circulator-kit page (or the homepage) and put a real number where the
  bracketed placeholder is.
- **Send in batches of ~10**, BCC'd, so replies are private. Personal
  follow-up gets way more engagement than a mass email.
- **Track who agrees** — when someone replies "I'm in," log them as a
  circulator on /admin/ (their commitment + neighborhood) so we can space out
  the Areas we're covering.
- **One-week followup**: anyone who didn't reply gets a single 2-sentence
  bump asking yes/no.
