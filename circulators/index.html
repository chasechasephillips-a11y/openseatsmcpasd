The pitch + the answers

What we're asking for: open 4 of MCPASD's 9 school board seats to any district resident, keep 5 anchored to Areas. Same board size. Voters still pick every seat — we're just widening who's allowed on the ballot.

Why now: the structure hasn't changed since 1963. Every other large WI district — Verona, Madison, Elmbrook, Appleton, Waukesha, Eau Claire, Sun Prairie, Stevens Point, Kenosha — has already moved to a hybrid or fully at-large model. Six straight years of cancelled spring primaries here. We're the outlier.

Who can sign?
Any registered MCPASD voter, 18+. One sig per person. Don't need kids in the district. Don't need to know what Area they live in.
"Is this partisan?"
No. No party, no PAC. Parents pay for printing themselves. It's about structure, not policy or candidates.
"Will my Area lose its voice?"
No. Every Area keeps a permanent anchor seat — one per Area, guaranteed. The petition adds four district-wide seats any qualified resident can run for. Your neighborhood's anchor stays intact; the district as a whole gains more ways to elect members focused on cross-district issues.
"Will any current board member be forced out?"
Nobody loses a seat. Conversion happens at natural re-election — April 2027, 2028, 2030. Current members can run for either the anchor or the open seats.
"Why hasn't this been changed before?"
It's never been put to a vote. Under WI law (§ 120.02(2)(a)) only a citizen petition + Annual Meeting vote can change apportionment. No school board or admin can do it. Nobody had organized one until now.
"Will it cost the district money?"
No. Same 9 seats, same election cycle, same costs. The only change is who can run.
"Is this even legal?"
Yes. Wis. Stat. § 120.02(2)(a) explicitly authorizes this — citizen petition + Annual Meeting vote. 500-signature minimum (we aim for 550 for cushion). Counsel-reviewed.
"I don't know my Area."
Doesn't matter — you can still sign. MCPASD has a school-boundary lookup if they want to know, but you don't need to write it down. That you don't know is kind of the point.
