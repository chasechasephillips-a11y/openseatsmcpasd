Who can vote

If you live here and you vote here, you can vote.

The MCPASD Annual Meeting is the only place this petition gets decided. It's not on a regular ballot — it happens in the room, by a vote of MCPASD electors physically present. Under Wis. Stat. § 120.08, that means anyone who meets all of the below.

You're eligible to vote if:

  • You live inside the Middleton-Cross Plains Area School District (Middleton, Cross Plains, Westport, Springfield, and parts of Berry, Vermont, Roxbury, and Madison).
  • You are 18 or older.
  • You are a US citizen and have lived at your address for at least 28 days.
  • You are physically present at the meeting on Sept 22.
  • You bring a photo ID (driver's license or Wisconsin ID).
What to expect

The meeting in four steps.

It's a Wisconsin school district Annual Meeting — long-standing, simple, public. The petition is one item on the agenda. Plan on ~30 minutes total.

01

Sign in

Show photo ID at the door. Get a paper ballot (if used). Find a seat.

02

Agenda + petition presented

The moderator reads the petition and explains the proposed change to the board apportionment.

03

Brief discussion

Anyone in the room can speak for or against — usually 1–2 minutes each. No filibustering allowed.

04

The vote

Show of hands or paper ballot, depending on the moderator. Simple majority of electors present decides.

Before you come

Quick tips to make it painless.

  • Arrive 15 minutes early so you can sign in, find parking, and grab a seat without rushing.
  • Bring a friend who's also an MCPASD elector. Every neighbor in the room is another vote in favor. About 30 people came in 2025; we need hundreds.
  • Have your photo ID ready when you walk in. Driver's license is easiest.
  • Pre-plan parking. The District Services Center has free parking on site. Use the main entrance off South Avenue.
  • Eat first. The meeting is short but you don't want to be hungry watching your democracy work.
  • Tell three more people in the next two weeks. Send them this URL: openseatsmcpasd.org/sept22.

The petition is the price of admission. The vote is at the meeting. Only people physically in the room can vote it through.
