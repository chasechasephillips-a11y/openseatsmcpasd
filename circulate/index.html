Why we're doing this: Right now you can only run for a school board seat if you live in one specific part of the district. We're asking neighbors to sign a petition to open some seats so anyone in the whole district can run — while every area still keeps its own seat. So every year there's a seat open to everyone, and voters get a real choice.

What we're doing & why tap to open

What am I asking people to do?
Sign a petition — with a real pen, on the official sheet. It asks the school district to let more people run for the school board. You're not asking for money and nothing is for sale.
Why does it matter?
Today, to run for a board seat you have to live inside one specific area's lines. Lots of great people can't even get on the ballot just because of which street their house is on. We want to remove restrictions that don't really matter, so more neighbors can serve.
Does every area still get a voice?
Yes. Every area keeps its own guaranteed seat — nobody loses representation. We're opening some of the seats on top of that, so there's always a seat anyone in the district can run for. The board stays the same size and no one is removed.
What do I say at the door?
“Hi! I'm helping with a local petition to let more people run for our school board — every area keeps its seat, we just open a few so anyone in the district can run. Would you take a look?” Then hand them the sheet or a door hanger. Smile, keep it short.
What if no one answers?
Leave a door hanger and tap “Left door hanger.” That still counts — they can scan the code and sign later.
What if they say no?
Say “No problem — have a great day!” and move on. Never argue. A friendly no is totally fine; you're just offering.
Stay safe: always go with your buddy or a grown-up, stay on sidewalks and driveways, and never go inside anyone's house. If anything feels off, leave and tell your adult.
