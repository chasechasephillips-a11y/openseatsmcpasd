Open Seats MCPASD

You're a ★ Helper ★

Thanks for helping make our schools better! Here's everything you need to know — it's easier than you think.

You're helping ask neighbors to sign a petition. A petition is just a paper where people write their name to say “yes, I agree!” When enough people sign, the grown-ups who run our schools have to listen.

What are we asking for?

The short version

We want to let more people run for the school board — the group of people who help make decisions about our schools. Right now, lots of good people aren't allowed to run, and we think that should change.

Why does it matter?

Right now there's a rule that's not fair

Today, you can only run for a school board seat if you live in one exact part of the district. That means a great person could be blocked from running just because of which street their house is on. We want to remove that rule for some of the seats.

Does every neighborhood still get a voice?

Yes! Every area keeps its own seat — nobody loses their spot. We're just opening up a few extra seats so that anyone in the whole district can run for them. That way voters get to pick from everyone who wants to help, not just a few people.

Are we taking anyone's seat away?

No. The board stays the same size, and nobody gets removed. We're just changing the rules about who is allowed to run.

What do I say at the door?

Just say something like this
“Hi! I'm helping with a petition to let more people run for our school board. Every neighborhood keeps its seat — we just want to open a few so anyone can run. Would you like to take a look?”

Then hand them the clipboard or a door hanger. Smile, be polite, and keep it short. You don't have to be an expert — if someone asks a hard question, just say: “That's a great question! My grown-up can explain, or you can read all about it at openseatsmcpasd.org.”

What if…?

…nobody answers the door?

No problem! Hang a door hanger on the handle and tap “Left door hanger” in the app. They can still scan it and sign later.

…they say “no thanks”?

Just smile and say “No problem — have a great day!” and go to the next house. Never argue. A friendly no is totally okay — you're just offering.

…they want to sign?

Awesome! Let your grown-up help them sign the official paper (a grown-up has to be the one in charge of the signatures). Then tap “They signed!” 🎉

How to log each house

  1. Open the log app on your phone (your grown-up will set it up).
  2. Type the house number and street.
  3. Tap the buttons for what happened (left a hanger, talked to someone, signed…).
  4. Hit the big “Save this stop” button — then on to the next house!
Open the Log App →or go to openseatsmcpasd.org/log

Staying safe — the most important part

  • Always go with your buddy or a grown-up.
  • Stay on sidewalks, driveways, and porches — never go inside anyone's house.
  • If anything feels weird or scary, walk away and tell your grown-up right away.
  • Take breaks, drink water, and have fun!
Every door you knock helps. Thank you for being awesome! 🌟
